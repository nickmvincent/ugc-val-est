Getting everyone on the same page

For a start, you should at least visualize your problems and proposed solutions, minimally in mock-ups or screenflows (doesn't need to be nice and complete). At least, these mock-ups can be used as a baseline or even part of the sign-off.

As for development "frameworks", there are many options available out there that are good in their own ways, you can use some ***ORM tools to speed up development

Quick & easy development :

Entity framework 6 - Developed by Microsoft themselves that boasts about fluent interaction with database & code-first approach, which promises minimal DB interaction for developers. The catch is, it gives poor performance compared to others

There are tons of alternatives like Nhibernate, nHydrate or micro ORMS like active records & dapper.net (by stackoverflow guys)

Determine your timeline

How long do you have to deliver the project? Do you want to include architecture patterns like ***http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dependency_injection or quality assurance processes unit-testing and continuous integration? Is it worth the budget? Do you have enough time?

Maintenance

Are you going to maintain this application? Try to do proper logging, exception handling before the poop hits the fan and get's everyone cursing.
